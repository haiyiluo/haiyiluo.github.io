Philosophical Context: John Rawls (1921-2002)

John Rawls was an American political philosopher known for his work "A Theory of Justice" (1971). His key concepts include:

  • Original Position & Veil of Ignorance: A thought experiment where people design society without knowing their place in it, ensuring fairness
  • Two Principles of Justice: Equal basic liberties for all, and social/economic inequalities must benefit the least advantaged
  • Justice as Fairness: Society should be structured to be fair to all members, with priority of liberty over economic considerations

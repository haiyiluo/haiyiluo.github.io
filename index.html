About This Project

This research project explores how social movements and protest activities spread through populations using a combination of computational modeling and qualitative interviews. By understanding the mechanisms of "resistance cascades," we can better comprehend the dynamics of collective action and social change.

The project uses an interdisciplinary approach, combining:

  • Computational Modeling: Mathematical simulations showing how protest participation spreads like an epidemic through social networks
  • In-depth Interviews: First-hand accounts from participants in resistance movements across different countries
  • Comparative Analysis: Understanding how different contexts shape movement dynamics and outcomes

Research Overview

This project examines the 2019 Hong Kong protests and other resistance movements to understand:

  • How individual decisions to participate are influenced by social networks and peer behavior
  • The role of "threshold effects" where critical mass triggers rapid mobilization
  • Differences between peaceful and confrontational tactics in movement dynamics
  • How state repression and violence affect protest trajectories
  • The relationship between civic duty, justice, and civil disobedience

Interview Sample

We conducted interviews with 12 participants from different backgrounds and locations:

  • Hong Kong (9 interviews): Direct participants in 2014 and 2019 movements, ranging from students to legal professionals
  • United Kingdom (1 interview): Diaspora perspective on solidarity movements
  • Mainland China (2 interviews): Observers and digital activists operating under different constraints

All interviews have been anonymized to protect participant identities.
